Unlike the manual I'm going to be defining certain terms first:

Channel: It is a term used by Element (The manufacturer) to describe the lights and the dimmer that controls them

Cue: It is a set of lights that is recorded and can be played back at a later date. Or what the stage lights look like.

The Central Information Area - CIA - What is displayed on the lower portion of the screen (below live)

How to even use the manual in the first place (Sticky 1 - pg2)

- The Face Panel buttons are indicated in brackets [example] - used with “Enter” or “Live” when a cue is currently running
    
- Any optional keys are shown with angle brackets <example> - used to display a cue
    
- Any buttons that are in the CIA (See terms list) are bracketed and bolded {Example}
    
- In the paper manual italics were used to indicate a reference to any other part of the text

Control keypad Layout (Sticky 3 - pg13)

- There are six kinds of keys on the lightboard - Display, softkeys, special functions controls, record targets (and similar commands), keypad and modifiers, and finally navigation.
    
- The keys that fall under “record targets” and the “numeric keypad and modifiers” will get the most use as they are responsible for setting and naming cues
    
- Special function keys are lucky if they will ever get used at all in there life - same with the soft and display
    
- The navigation keys will be used sparingly.
    

-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Creating/ Dealing with Show Files (Sticky 4 - pg29)

- To create a new show navigate to file tab, then hit new than select
    
- A show cannot be named until the show has been saved, until then it will remain “Untitled”
    
- To select a specific show file (Something that we should have taught Nigel) go to file, open, then find desired file.
    
- Please be sure to save the show after making edits
    

-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Selecting Channels (Sticky 5 - pg 41)

- “Thru” key can also been used in several cases
    
- [5] [Enter] - selects channel 5
    
- [5] [+] [7] [Enter] - selects non-consecutive channel 5 through 7
    
- [5] [thru] [9] [enter] - selects channels 5 through 9
    
- [2] [thru] [8] [-] [5] [Enter] - selects a range of channels 2 through 8 excluding 5
    
- [-] [6] [Enter] - removes channel 6 from current list of channels
    
- [+] [1] [Enter] - Adds channel 1 from current list of channels

The Basics of Cues (Sticky 7 - pg58) (Make Abby check/add to)

- The numbering of the cues can be from .01 - 10,000
    
- They are the pre sets for the lighting on the stage that we cycle through during the show
    
- The submasters are used to get the desired lighting affect - then hit [record] [cue] [#]
    
- If there is anything you take away from this godforsaken manual is how to record/ the importance of cues and keeping them organized
    

-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Why the Channel Numbers are colored as they are during cues/ use of record (Sticky 8 - pg59)

- Blue, the intensity has increased from the previous cue
    
- Green, the intensity has decreased from previous cue
    
- Magenta, the intensity level has stayed the same
    
- White, level has been blocked (Don't worry I didn't cover that)
    
- Similar to the last - [record] <cue> [5] [enter] - how to record cue

Cue Attributes (Sticky 9 - pg63) (Basically none of this applies to us but I thought it was cool)

- This can be recorded when a cue is initially written or at a later date
    
- These attributes can be things such as having them loop and link
    
- Created using the follow key
    
- Can be removed with the [x] key
    

-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Modifying cues live (Sticky 10 - pg68)

- [Recall from], [copy to] , {replace with} and {move to} are used to create and edit cues
    
- [At] [Enter] is equivalent to recall from previous cue
    
- Can also be used to set channel parameters by calling them out specifically 
    
- Another alternative is rewriting the cue in its entirety
    
- [Record] [enter] [enter] - will rewrite a active cue in its entirety to current stage settings
    
- [Record] [Cue] [x] [Enter] [Enter] - will replace any data in cue “x” with current stage values

Blind Mode/ Live Mode (Sticky 12 - pg 72+77)

- It is activated by pressing the [Blind] key under display on the keyboard
    
- Any changes that are made in blind mode will not affect current stage state
    
- This is quite useless to us so actually read the manual if you want more information
    

-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Cue Keybinds (Sticky 13 pg79)

- [Go to cue] [Enter] - replays current cue
    
- [go to cue] [Next/Last] [Enter] - Takes you to next/previous cue in active list
    
- [go to cue] [2] [Time] [1] [Enter] - takes you to cue 2 in 1 second
    
- [go to cue] [next] [time] [3] [enter] - takes you to next cue in selected list in 3 seconds

Color indicators (sticky 15 pg124)

- When outlined in gold - channel is selected
    
- When number is blue - the light has gotten brighter since previous cue
    
- When outlined in green - The light has gotten dimmer since previous cue
    
- When purple - the light has stayed the same since previous cue
    
- When yellow - the light value has been changed by the submaster
    
- *again knowing this has no bearing on one's ability to operate the light board, it's just a fun fact*
